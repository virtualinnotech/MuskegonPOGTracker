🔑 The 5-Level POG Progression Scale

Every student is on a growth journey. These levels describe where they are — not a ceiling, but a starting point for the next conversation.

1
EMERGING
Building awareness. Introduce, explore, set first goals.
2
DEVELOPING
Growing with support. Practice, feedback, and scaffolding.
3
PROFICIENT
Consistent demonstration. Real-world connection.
4
ADVANCED
Self-directed. Mentoring peers. Stretch challenges.
5
TRANSFORMATIVE
Exemplifies and elevates others. Community impact.
💡 Use the Skills Lab to understand why a student is at their current level — and what motivates their next step. A student at Level 2 who rates related skills "Always" has everything they need to grow; they just need the right opportunity.
📋 The rubric below describes observable behaviors for each POG across the 5-level progression: Emerging → Developing → Proficient → Advanced → Transformative. Teachers use this to assign POG levels each marking period. Update descriptors with exact language from your Muskegon DRAFT spreadsheet.

School Year Transition

At the start of each new school year, advance all students one grade. Their POG levels, evidence, Xello data, and notes all carry forward with them. Grade 12 students are flagged as Graduated rather than deleted.

Current roster: — students

Marking Period Workflow

  1. Students complete Xello Skills Lab (once at start of year, re-take optional)
  2. Teacher observes student behaviors and assigns a POG level (1 Emerging → 5 Transformative) each marking period
  3. Open the Student Profile to see the full crosswalk progression view
  4. Use the Teacher Prompt to guide a 5-minute coaching conversation
  5. Log notes and set a goal for next marking period

💬 Sample Teacher Prompts

"I see you rated Teamwork as something you want to use often, but your Collaborator POG is still developing. Let's set one goal for how you can practice that this quarter."
"You consistently demonstrate strong organization skills — your Persistent Professional POG is one of your highlights. Have you thought about roles where that skill is central?"
"You're doing great in group projects, but you rated Teamwork as Never in Skills Lab. That's interesting — tell me more about what kind of work environment sounds ideal to you."

Xello Skills Lab — 35 Skills Reference

Students select their top skills from this list in Xello. Import them via CSV at the start of the year, or enter them manually via a student's profile. Selected skills are highlighted in the POG crosswalk to guide coaching.

📌 Skills Lab removes performance anxiety by asking about preferences, not current ability. This makes it ideal for honest self-reflection.
